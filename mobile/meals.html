Breakfast (Low-sodium):

Greek Yogurt with Berries and Seeds

Protein: 20 g

A 7-ounce container of plain, nonfat Greek yogurt contains around 150 calories and 20 grams of protein.2 USDA FoodData Central. Yogurt, Greek, plain, low-fat. Boost the nutritional value even more by adding some of your favorite toppings. Berries contribute fiber and antioxidants, while nuts or seeds (like chia, ground flax, or hemp) provide healthy fats, fiber, and extra protein for a well-rounded breakfast.

Breakfest

Edamame and Veggies Breakfast Stir-Fry

Protein: 18 g

Edamame are young soybeans, generally found in the grocery store's frozen section. They provide around 18 grams of protein and 8 grams of fiber per cup, making them a filling, egg-free breakfast option.

An edamame stir-fry is just as good for breakfast as at any other time of the day. Toss your edamame into a quick sauté with colorful veggies like bell peppers, spinach, and mushrooms. For bold flavor, add garlic, ginger, and a splash of low-sodium soy sauce or tamari. Serve over brown rice or quinoa or with a side of whole-grain avocado toast.

Breakfest

Lentil Breakfast Hash

Protein: 18 g

Lentils provide a great high-protein base for a savory breakfast plate. One cup of cooked lentils contains 18 grams of protein and 230 calories, plus 16 grams of fiber to help keep you full all morning.

To make a breakfast hash using lentils, sauté cooked lentils with diced potatoes, bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, and your favorite spices or herbs. Top it with some fresh salsa or guacamole to taste, and enjoy it with whole grain toast or a muffin.

Breakfest (Diabetic-friendly)

Sweet Potato hash and Eggs with Avocado Drizzle

A colorful mix of sweet potatoes, bell peppers and onion are tossed with smoky spices for a delicious hash, and eggs are cooked right in the pan for a healthy breakfast. Keeping the yolks soft lets them form a rich sauce that drips over and flavors the vegetables, while a creamy avocado drizzle adds a smooth finish and balances out the flavors beautifully.

The dish comes together quickly, and you can even prep the vegetables the night before if you’d like. It’s a satisfying way to start your day with an excellent balance of protein, healthy fats and fiber.

Breakfest

Eggs Benedict Breakfest Salad

This high-protein salad has everything that makes eggs Benedict a breakfast favorite – including a creamy lemon dressing that stands in for hollandaise sauce. It’s super satisfying and a really great way to start your day.

The combination of leafy greens and eggs provides a boost of choline, an essential nutrient for brain health and cognitive function. Pair it with a big mug of Earl Grey tea for a well-rounded meal.

Breakfest

Shakshuka

Shakshuka has risen in popularity around the world, and for good reason. Originally from Tunisia, this flavorful dish of eggs cooked in a spicy tomato sauce is tasty, healthy and a breeze to make.

It’s a terrific one-skillet meal you can serve right from the pan and it lends itself to variation. Try this international superstar for breakfast, lunch or dinner. Packed with protein and lycopene-rich tomatoes, shakshuka is a balanced meal that delivers steady energy — making it a great option for those managing diabetes.

Lunch

Zucchini-Chickpea Veggie Burgers with Tahini-Ranch Sauce

This veggie burger is one you'll want to make again and again. Savory chickpea and zucchini patties are seasoned with garlic, onion, and warm spices, then lightly pan-seared until the outside turns golden and crisp. Each patty stays tender on the inside, giving it a satisfying bite without feeling heavy. On top, a creamy tahini-ranch sauce adds a cool, herby flavor that pairs perfectly with the warm patty.

Fresh tomato slices bring natural sweetness and juiciness, while peppery arugula adds a light crunch and extra vitamins. Everything is tucked inside a whole-grain bun for added fiber, making this meal filling and heart-healthy. This homemade veggie burger is high in plant-based protein, full of vegetables, and low in saturated fat — a great option for anyone who wants nutritious flavor without giving up taste.

Dinner

Sesame-Garlic Beef & Broccoli with Whole-Wheat Noodles

A savory marinade made with garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and sesame oil adds rich flavor to tender sirloin steak pieces in this healthy beef-and-broccoli stir-fry. The fresh aromatics give the meat a warm, balanced taste, while the sesame oil adds a nutty finish. As the steak cooks, the marinade helps keep each bite juicy and full of flavor without needing extra salt.

We recommend partially freezing the beef before slicing, because it firms up the meat and makes it easier to cut into thin, even strips. This allows the steak to cook quickly and pair well with crisp broccoli. Served over brown rice or whole-grain noodles, this dish creates a balanced meal that delivers protein, fiber, and important nutrients in every bite.
